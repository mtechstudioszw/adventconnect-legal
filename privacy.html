Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 June 2026 · Effective on first use of the app

This Privacy Policy explains what information Advent Connect ZW ("the App", "we", "us") collects, how it is stored, who it is shared with, and the level of access different parties have. The App is operated by Tanatswa Michael Mikuwa, a sole developer (trading as MyTech Studios ZW) based in Zimbabwe. Contact: tanatswamichaelmikuwa@gmail.com. It describes the application as implemented in its source code. Where behaviour could not be determined from the code, it is marked "To be confirmed" and listed in Section 13.

Important — messaging is not end-to-end encrypted. Messages, images and voice notes you send are stored on our servers in readable form and are protected by access controls, not end-to-end encryption. See Section 8.

1. Who provides the service / data processors

The App is built on the following providers, which process and store data on our behalf:

  • Supabase — database (PostgreSQL), authentication, file storage, realtime, and server functions. The substantive data described below is stored on Supabase infrastructure in Ireland (EU region).
  • Google / Firebase — Firebase Cloud Messaging (push notifications), Firebase Analytics (usage events), Firebase Crashlytics (crash diagnostics), and Google Sign-In (optional login).
  • Apple — "Sign in with Apple" (iOS only; only where enabled).

The code contains no advertising SDKs and no payment processing inside the App.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Account & identity

  • Email address and password (passwords are handled and hashed by Supabase Auth — we never store them in readable form), or a Google / Apple sign-in identifier if you use social login.
  • Full name and username.
  • Date of birth (used to derive your age; see 2.7).
  • Profile photo and cover photo.
  • Short bio / "about" text.
  • Home church, and your province and city (as you enter them).

2.2 Content you create

  • Feed posts (text and/or photo).
  • Stories (photo with optional caption; automatically expire ~24 hours after posting).
  • Prayer requests (text; marked public or anonymous).
  • Marketplace listings (title, price, description, photos) and job posts.
  • Events you create (and RSVPs you make).
  • Comments, likes/reactions, and friend connections.
  • Churches you follow, and — if you apply to manage a congregation — your church-admin application, which includes an appointment-letter file and the applicant name and phone number you provide.

2.3 Messages

  • One-to-one and group chat messages: text, images, and voice-note audio.
  • Message reactions, edits, "forwarded" flags, replies, and read / delivery receipts.
  • Messages you "star" (saved for yourself).

2.4 Device & technical data

  • A Firebase Cloud Messaging push token, stored on your profile so we can deliver notifications.
  • Network connectivity state (online/offline), used on-device.
  • Locally cached content (recent feed, inbox, messages) stored on your device.

2.5 Usage analytics (Firebase Analytics)

We log a limited set of events, associated with your user identifier: screen views, and the events church_followed, message_sent (a source label only — no message content), prayer_posted (visibility setting), marketplace_contact, event_rsvp, church_claimed, job_posted, and seller_applied. Standard Firebase Analytics device/app metadata also applies.

2.6 Crash diagnostics (Firebase Crashlytics)

When the App crashes, Crashlytics collects a crash stack trace, device and operating-system information, and your user identifier.

2.7 Date of birth & age

Your date of birth is stored in your device's secure storage and used to compute your age. Whether your age is shown to other users is controlled by your "Show age" privacy setting.

2.8 Location

With your permission, the App requests your precise (GPS) location only to sort nearby churches by distance. Per the source code, the distance is computed on your device; your coordinates are not transmitted to or stored on our servers.

2.9 Reports

If you report content (a message, group, post, listing, profile, etc.), we record the content type, its identifier, the reason, and that you submitted it, for moderation.

4. Where data is stored

  • On Supabase (PostgreSQL + file Storage): all account, profile, content, messaging and media data. Some image buckets are publicly readable by design (profile photos, post photos, story photos, product photos, event flyers); chat images and voice notes are kept in private buckets accessible only via short-lived signed URLs to permitted participants.
  • On your device: authentication tokens (in the operating-system secure keychain via flutter_secure_storage), your date of birth, and a local cache of recent content/media.

6. Who can access your data (access levels)

  • You — your own data and content shared with you, enforced by database Row-Level Security.
  • Other users — only what you make visible according to the App's visibility rules (e.g., public vs friends-only posts, public vs anonymous prayers, church membership).
  • Our servers / administrators — backend functions and administration use a privileged database credential that can read stored data. This means authorised administrators can technically access stored content, including messages (messaging is not end-to-end encrypted). A super-admin role exists for moderation: reviewing reports, banning accounts, sending broadcast notices, and approving church-admin / seller / news submissions.

7. Blocking

If you block another user, that user can no longer see your profile, posts, stories or prayers, and their messages to you are not delivered.

8. Encryption model

  • In transit: connections to Supabase and Google services use TLS/HTTPS.
  • Authentication tokens: stored in your device's secure keychain.
  • Messages & media: NOT end-to-end encrypted. They are stored server-side in readable form and protected by access controls (Row-Level Security), not by end-to-end encryption. Administrators with database access can read them, and the server reads message content to generate push-notification previews and to support moderation.
  • At rest: data is hosted by Supabase in Ireland (EU); provider-level encryption-at-rest applies per Supabase's platform.

9. Data retention

  • Stories and their photos are deleted automatically (rows daily; photo files within ~48 hours of expiry).
  • Notifications are pruned automatically (read ones after ~45 days, all after ~90 days); resolved reports after ~60 days.
  • Other content remains until you delete it or delete your account.

10. Deleting your account

You can request account deletion from within the App (Settings) or via our account-deletion page: Delete my account. The exact scope of what is deleted versus retained is listed for confirmation in Section 13.

Subject to applicable law (including Zimbabwe's Cyber and Data Protection Act, and the EU GDPR where it applies given our Ireland/EU hosting), you may also request access to, correction of, or erasure of your personal data, object to certain processing, or request a copy of your data, by emailing tanatswamichaelmikuwa@gmail.com. You can disable notification, location, camera, microphone and photo permissions in your device settings at any time.
